    • Welcome to Billify

    Your Spotify Listening History, Served as a Receipt

    Log in with Spotify

    Billify is a free Spotify analytics tool that connects to your Spotify account and displays your listening history as a stylized receipt. Unlike Spotify Wrapped, which only shows your year-end stats, Billify lets you explore your top tracks, artists, and genres across three time periods any time of the year.

    Top Tracks

    See exactly which songs you have been listening to the most over the last month, last 6 months, or last year. Ranked by play frequency using Spotify's own listening data.

    Top Artists

    Find out which artists dominate your listening habits. See their popularity score and how they rank in your personal music taste across different time windows.

    Top Genres

    Discover the genres that define your music taste. See what percentage of your top artists fall into each genre category, from pop and hip-hop to indie and classical.

    Listening Stats

    Go deeper with audio feature analysis. Billify uses Spotify's audio analysis API to calculate your average danceability, energy, tempo, happiness, acousticness, and instrumentalness scores.

    What is Billify?

    Billify is a Spotify analytics web app built with Python and Flask, using the Spotify Web API to pull your personal listening data. It formats your music stats in a unique receipt-style layout, making it easy to read and share. You can view your top 10, 20, or 30 items and switch between short term (last month), medium term (last 6 months), and long term (last year) data.

    Spotify's audio features API provides detailed metrics for every track, including danceability (how suitable a track is for dancing), valence (musical happiness), energy (intensity and activity), acousticness, instrumentalness, and tempo in BPM. Billify aggregates these across your top tracks to give you a unique fingerprint of your music taste.

    How is Billify Different from Spotify Wrapped?

    Spotify Wrapped is only available once a year, at the end of December. Billify gives you the same kind of insight any time you want it. You can check your stats in January, mid-year, or whenever you are curious about how your music taste has shifted. Billify also goes deeper than Wrapped with audio feature analysis that Spotify does not surface in its own app.

    Is Billify Free?

    Yes. Billify is completely free to use. It uses Spotify's official Web API and only requests read access to your listening history. No data is stored on any server. Your Spotify token is kept only in your browser session and expires after one hour.
